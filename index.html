The Gap

Most boutique hotels and restaurants are closer to that feeling than they think.

They have soul. They have intention. But something isn't quite landing — and they can't see it because they're too close to it every day.

It might be the way the entrance smells. The pause before a guest gets acknowledged. The bathroom that doesn't match the rest of the experience. The music that's slightly off. The staff who are trying but don't quite know how to make someone feel seen.

These are the things guests feel — and talk about — even when they can't articulate exactly why.
